What is it? What is its purpose?
The nervous system is a giant interlocking system of nerves that control the human body. It sends and receives electrical signals from the body to allow it to move, and the nerves are the path to which these electrical signals travel on. It is composed of 2 main parts, the Central Nervous System (CNS), encompasses the brain and the spinal cord, and the Peripheral Nervous System (PNS), which encompasses the Somatic Nervous System (SNS) and the Automatic Nervous System (ANS). The nervous system is probably the most important organ system in our body. It is basically the command or overseer of the human body. The nervous system is responsible for allowing us to move, think, and interact with the world, all through the power of nerves, hence the name. It controls every part of the body, and is probably the one allowing us to think and feel.
